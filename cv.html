Cover Letter

Application for position of Community Support Representative

August 21, 2015

Dear Patreon,

One morning about a year ago, I found myself sipping a strong cup of coffee catching up on my daily RSS feeds. I came across an article detailing a new start up named Patreon, a write-up which left me both excited and, in a sense, relieved. The internet has always been a place funded and upheld by advertising, just as television before it. However, an unforeseen facet of this network is its ability to create a globally decentralized “Medici Family,” with websites like Patreon being its Florence. I believe that this style of funding will be essential in maintaining the diversity and democracy that the internet has brought to the art world. And so, I found my way to your jobs postings happy to see all of your smiling faces and bios and exhilarated by the list of open positions. After reading through the various descriptions, this is one of the positions for which I felt I would be a suitable candidate.

I graduated with a B.Sc. in Mathematics, a field very closely connected to Computer Science. During the course of my degree, I worked hands on with various programming languages including Maple, Matlab and Latex. In addition, for the last 6 months I have been focusing on more pervasive languages such as Python and HTML/CSS/JS using the abundance of sources on the internet as a guide while practicing with various personal projects to hone my skills. I believe that this type of background would allow me to serve as capable bridge between the users and the engineering team. My insight could be constructive in utilizing feedback effectively to help Patreon continue to improve.

I was employed with my university's outreach program in which I would teach math concepts to promising S.T.E.M. students. The topics we covered, if treated incorrectly, could be dull and boring to a class of high schoolers, but this experience allowed me to learn how to verbally shroud these subjects in such a way that I could teach the kids without them even realizing! After my first year with high school students, I was then asked to help with larger freshman tutorial classes. Here my technical communication was put to the test, with one on one personal explanations. This experience helped my communication skills to further grow.

So why did I wait a year to apply? At the beginning of my last year of University, my family's business caught on fire. We were faced with a year long absence which we feared could deteriorate our customer base. However, this event also gave us a rare chance to rejuvenate our business and return stronger than before. When I returned from school I spent the remaining 3 months working on the construction, and 6 months getting the business on it's feet, needing to learn entire systems and invent new solutions every step of the way. Being there for that experience showed me what really goes into starting a business from the ground up. I'm confident I would be able to channel this experience into my work at a start up like Patreon because I firmly believe in its goals. Thank you for your time and consideration, I look forward to hearing from you.

Regards,
